Counseling and Psychological Services

Counseling and Psychological Services (CAPS) offers a confidential, safe, and welcoming environment where students can seek comprehensive mental health support. Whether you're dealing with stress, anxiety, depression, or personal challenges, CAPS is dedicated to your well-being. The staff consists of trained mental health professionals who provide a range of services, including individual counseling, group therapy, crisis intervention, and wellness workshops. Every session is treated with strict confidentiality, ensuring a space where you can openly discuss your concerns without judgment. Your emotional and psychological health is their top priority, and CAPS is committed to offering the personalized care and resources necessary to help you thrive during your time at the university.

Support

Individual Counseling Support

Individual Peer Counseling (IPC) is designed to offer a new way to meet the needs of students across the University of Michigan. We are here to support student mental health and offer students a place to vent. Peer counselors are trained in active listening by professional staff here at CAPS. The IPC program connects students with someone of a similar age, interest and background who can serve as a resource or simply someone to “rant” to. While Peer Counselors will keep all information private, IPC is not a substitute for confidential therapy provided by a licensed mental health CAPS staff member.

Group Conselling Support

Group participation requires completing a 20-30-minute brief consultation appointment and/or a 20-30-minute pre-group screening with the group facilitator. CAPS is committed to providing clinical and supportive group services that are responsive to the needs of our students.
